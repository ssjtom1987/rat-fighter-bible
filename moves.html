Combat System

Universal Fighting Framework

The combat system is built around simple universal controls combined with unique character abilities. Every fighter shares the same core mechanics, making the roster easy to learn while allowing each character to feel distinct through specials, transformations, finishers, stats and playstyle.

Controls

Standard Controls

Light Punch

Fast combo starter with low damage and quick recovery.

Heavy Punch

Slower strike with higher damage and greater stun potential.

Light Kick

Quick low-risk kick used for pressure, pokes and interrupting movement.

Heavy Kick

Powerful kick with longer reach, stronger knockback and slower recovery.

Special

Directional inputs combined with attack buttons activate each fighter's unique special moves.

Super

Consumes meter to unleash a high-impact cinematic attack unique to each fighter.

Standing Attacks

Jab

Fastest attack. Low damage, quick recovery, useful for interrupting opponents and starting combos.

Cross / Straight Punch

Slower than the jab but hits harder. A reliable mid-range strike for basic damage and pressure.

Uppercut

Anti-air attack designed to punish jumping opponents. Higher risk, higher reward.

High Kick

Longer range standing kick. Useful for keeping opponents away but slower on recovery.

Low Kick

Fast poke attack aimed at the legs. Useful for checking movement and setting up pressure.

Jumping Attacks

Flying Punch

A jumping punch used to approach from the air and start pressure on landing.

Jump Kick

A stronger aerial attack with better reach. Ideal for jump-ins, but vulnerable to uppercuts.

Crouching Attacks

Crouching Jab

Quick low-position strike. Good for close pressure and stopping aggressive opponents.

Crouching Punch

A stronger crouching strike with slightly more commitment than crouching jab.

Sweep

Low sweeping attack that knocks the opponent down. Great for creating space and setting up pressure.

Throws

Forward Throw

Close-range grab that throws the opponent forward. Useful for breaking defensive players.

Back Throw

Close-range grab that swaps sides and throws the opponent behind the fighter.

Defensive Options

Block

Hold back to block standing and mid attacks. Reduces or prevents damage depending on the final system.

Crouch Block

Used to block low attacks. Leaves the fighter vulnerable to overhead-style attacks if added later.

Air Block

Optional mechanic. Allows limited blocking while airborne, but may be removed if it makes jump-ins too safe.

Movement

Walk

Basic forward and backward movement. Speed varies by character.

Crouch

Lower stance used for defence, low attacks and avoiding some high strikes.

Jump

Vertical jump used for avoiding low attacks and setting up aerial attacks.

Forward Jump

Jump toward the opponent to close distance and start pressure.

Back Jump

Jump away from the opponent to escape pressure or bait attacks.

Universal Specials

Every character has five unique character-specific mechanics layered on top of the universal move set.

Signature Special

The fighter's main special move. This should define their core personality and fighting style.

Secondary Special

A second unique move used for utility, mix-ups, combos, movement, zoning or defence.

Super Move

A high-impact cinematic attack that costs meter and delivers major damage or momentum.

Transformation

A temporary powered-up state that changes visuals and boosts certain abilities.

Finisher

An over-the-top end-of-match cinematic move used to finish the opponent in dramatic fashion.

Resources

Super Meter

Landing attacks, taking damage, blocking pressure and extending combos gradually fills the Super Meter.

Gain Meter

Attack, defend and survive to build meter throughout the match.

Spend Meter

Use meter to activate devastating Super Moves unique to every fighter.

Strategy

Choosing when to spend meter can decide an entire match. Save it, spend it, or regret it.

Rage Mode

Rage Mode activates when a fighter drops to low health. The screen freezes, the announcer calls “Rage Mode!”, and the fighter gains access to their Transformation.

Trigger

Suggested activation point: around 25% health remaining.

Purpose

Creates dramatic comeback moments and gives each fighter a unique final-phase identity.

Risk

Transformations should be powerful but not automatic wins. Some may boost damage while weakening defence.

Roster Balance

Character Archetypes

Rushdown

Fast, relentless pressure fighters that excel at staying close.

Power

Heavy hitters with slower movement but devastating attacks.

Technical

Characters that reward precision, spacing and advanced execution.

Grappler

Throw specialists capable of enormous damage at close range.

Wildcard

Unpredictable fighters with unusual mechanics and unconventional attacks.

Boss

Powerful opponents designed to provide the greatest challenge in Arcade Mode.

Design Notes

  • Basic attacks should be shared by all fighters for consistency.
  • Characters should feel different through speed, damage, range, recovery and special effects.
  • Uppercut should reliably counter jump-ins.
  • Sweep should cause knockdown and create pressure opportunities.
  • Throws should stop players from blocking forever.
  • Super Meter should reward both aggression and survival.
  • Rage Mode should create comeback potential without becoming an automatic win button.
  • Transformations should be visually obvious and tied strongly to each character's personality.
  • Finishers should be dramatic, funny, violent in a cartoon way, and memorable.
