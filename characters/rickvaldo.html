Biography

Rickvaldo was born and raised in Liverpool, but after watching one too many western films as a kid, he became convinced he was destined for life on the American frontier.

He wears cowboy boots everywhere, owns several identical hats, insists everyone calls him “partner” and genuinely believes his thick Scouse accent sounds Texan.

Nobody has ever agreed.

Years of pub fights, warehouse scraps and complete overconfidence have given Rickvaldo a surprisingly effective fighting style built around haymakers, wrestling throws, imaginary gunfights and total refusal to accept reality.

When the King of Fists invitation arrived, Rickvaldo assumed the greatest rodeo in history had finally come calling. Nobody had the heart to tell him the tournament was not in Texas.

Special Moves

Signature Special: Rodeo Rush

Rickvaldo charges shoulder-first across the arena while yelling “YEEHAW!” in the least Texan accent imaginable.

Secondary Special: Lasso Lariat

He swings an imaginary lasso before flattening the opponent with a wild clothesline. There is no rope. Nobody knows what he is swinging.

Super Move: High Noon Showdown

Rickvaldo slowly walks toward the opponent pretending to holster invisible revolvers, pauses dramatically, then simply punches them extremely hard.

Transformation: Cowboy Fever

Rickvaldo becomes completely convinced he is the fastest gunslinger alive, increasing his damage, armour and forward momentum.

Finisher: Sheriff’s Last Warning

The screen fades to sepia, old western music begins, and the opponent prepares for a dramatic duel. Rickvaldo headbutts them unconscious.

Arcade Ending

After defeating Deronimo, Rickvaldo finally books the trip he has talked about his entire life.

He arrives in Texas expecting to be welcomed by fellow cowboys. Instead, he is immediately mistaken for a tourist from Liverpool.

After ten minutes of explaining that he is “basically local,” Rickvaldo wanders into a line dancing bar where he somehow wins the annual rodeo championship by complete accident.

The locals never understand a word he says.

Rickvaldo returns home wearing an even bigger hat, fully convinced he saved the Wild West.
