Character Profile

Rickvaldo

The Last Cowboy

Born and raised in Liverpool, Rickvaldo somehow became convinced he was destined to live life as an American gunslinger. Whether he's walking through the city centre, ordering a pint or queueing in Tesco, he does it wearing cowboy boots, a battered hat and enough confidence to make people question reality.

Biography

Rickvaldo grew up obsessed with old western films. Somewhere between Clint Eastwood, John Wayne and far too many spaghetti westerns, something clicked. He stopped pretending. Instead, he fully embraced the fantasy. Today he genuinely believes he is the last great cowboy. He insists on greeting everyone with "Howdy, partner," challenges strangers to imaginary duels and somehow thinks his thick Scouse accent sounds unmistakably Texan. Nobody has ever agreed. Years of settling arguments in pubs and clubs have given Rickvaldo an unpredictable but surprisingly effective fighting style based around wild haymakers, wrestling throws and absolute self-belief. When the King of Fists invitation arrives, Rick assumes the greatest rodeo in history has finally come calling. Nobody tells him otherwise.

Special Moves

🤠 Rodeo Rush

Rick charges shoulder-first across the screen shouting "YEEEEHAAAW!" before smashing into the opponent.

🤠 Lasso Lariat

Rick spins an imaginary lasso before clotheslining his opponent. There is no rope. Nobody knows what he's swinging.

🤠 High Noon Showdown

Rick slowly walks toward his opponent pretending to holster invisible revolvers. After an awkward pause... He simply punches them incredibly hard.

🤠 Cowboy Fever

Rick becomes completely convinced he's the fastest gunslinger alive. Damage, armour and movement speed all increase dramatically.

🤠 Sheriff's Last Warning

The screen fades to sepia. Old western music begins. The opponent prepares for a dramatic duel... Rick headbutts them unconscious.

Arcade Ending

After defeating Deronimo, Rickvaldo finally fulfils his lifelong dream and travels to Texas. He expects to be welcomed by fellow cowboys. Instead, everyone assumes he's an unusually enthusiastic tourist from Liverpool. Unable to understand a word he says, the locals simply smile politely while Rick spends the entire holiday convinced he's "finally home." He returns to England wearing an even bigger hat.
