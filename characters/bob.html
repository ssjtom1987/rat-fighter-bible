Biography

Bob is a walking contradiction: a war veteran, a professional layabout, a beer philosopher, and one of the most dangerous men ever to be underestimated.

Though almost always seen in his battered wheelchair, the truth is stranger than the rumour. His legs work perfectly well. He simply prefers to save them for kicking people through walls.

Behind the smoke, drink and total refusal to behave like a normal human being is a man hardened by decades of combat, loss and survival. Bob has taken punishment that would flatten younger fighters, then asked if anyone had a lighter.

Years ago, one of Deronimo's weapons operations supplied arms that led to the deaths of several of Bob's closest brothers-in-arms. The world moved on. Bob did not.

When the King of Fists invitation arrived, Bob accepted for one simple reason: some debts can only be settled face to face.

Special Moves

Signature Special: Beer Bottle Bash

Bob smashes a beer bottle over the opponent before following up with a savage wheelchair-assisted shoulder barge.

Secondary Special: Bong Rip

Bob takes a heavy hit and exhales a cloud of smoke, briefly confusing the opponent and slowing their reactions.

Super Move: Rolling Thunder

Bob launches himself across the arena in his wheelchair like a missile, bouncing the opponent off every surface in sight.

Transformation: Veteran Mode

Bob stands up from the wheelchair. The arena goes silent. His speed increases, his strikes become brutal, and everyone realises the chair was never the dangerous part.

Finisher: One Last Ride

Bob whistles in an old tank, climbs aboard, and runs the opponent clean through the battlefield before lighting a cigarette.

Arcade Ending

After Deronimo falls, officials arrive to offer Bob medals, interviews and a place in history.

He refuses all of it.

Instead, he returns home with Kel, parks his wheelchair beside the porch and cracks open a cold beer.

For the first time in years, the ghosts are quiet.

Kel asks if he feels better.

Bob takes a long drink, looks out at the sunset and shrugs.

"Aye. But I still reckon I could've taken the rat in two rounds."
